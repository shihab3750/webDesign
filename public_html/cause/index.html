Why Care

Climate change in Bangladesh is not just a local issue but a global one that affects us all.

Bangladesh is one of the most vulnerable countries to the impacts of climate change, with rising sea levels, extreme weather events, and changing rainfall patterns posing significant threats to the country's infrastructure, economy, and people.The impacts of climate change in Bangladesh are already being felt, with millions of people being displaced due to floods, landslides, and other climate hazards. The country's agriculture sector is also being severely impacted, with changing rainfall patterns and temperature increases leading to reduced crop yields and food insecurity. As a global community, we must all care about climate change in Bangladesh because it has significant implications for the rest of the world. The impacts of climate change in Bangladesh are not confined to its borders but have regional and global consequences, such as increased migration and potential conflicts over resources. Furthermore, Bangladesh is a developing country with limited resources and capacity to address the impacts of climate change. As a global community, we have a responsibility to support vulnerable countries like Bangladesh in adapting to the impacts of climate change and reducing greenhouse gas emissions. Ignoring the impacts of climate change in Bangladesh also undermines efforts to achieve the global goal of limiting global temperature increases to below 1.5Â°C. Bangladesh's vulnerability to climate change highlights the urgent need for global action to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and transition to a low-carbon economy. In conclusion, climate change in Bangladesh is not just a local issue but a global one that affects us all. We must all care about the impacts of climate change in Bangladesh and take urgent action to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and support vulnerable countries in adapting to the impacts of climate change.
